# Notes

My model uses prosodic and phonetic decay signals from the final 1.5 seconds before a pause: the delta (slope) of pitch (F0) to detect falling intonation, RMS energy delta to catch fading volume, and the mean/variance of 13 MFCCs for phonetic structure. The in-sample score.py number looked strong (100 ms), but grouped 5-fold cross-validation (held-out by turn_id) tells a more honest story: out-of-fold AUC is only 0.645, and the held-out score is English ~1.3 s / Hindi ~0.85 s @ 5% cutoffs — a modest win over the 1600 ms baseline for English and essentially none for Hindi. Regularizing the forest (shallower trees, larger leaves) didn't close that gap, so the pooled mean/delta statistics over the window just don't carry much signal that transfers to unseen turns, rather than this being pure variance/overfitting. The model also likely struggles in high background noise, since `librosa.pyin` can misread noise as voiced pitch and skew the delta features, and may cut off speakers who trail off without a pitch drop. If I had one more day I'd spend it on the honest-generalization gap first: try per-frame time-series features (a small LSTM/GRU reading the window directly instead of squashing it to means/variances) or simpler, more robust cues (e.g. plain energy trend without pitch, which is less noise-sensitive) and re-check both against the same grouped-CV harness before trusting any score.
